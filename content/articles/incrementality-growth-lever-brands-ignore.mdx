Incrementality answers the question that attribution cannot: what would have happened if we had not spent the money?

That question is uncomfortable because it can reveal that some highly credited activity is mostly following existing demand.

## The missing counterfactual

Most marketing reporting observes what happened after exposure. Incrementality compares that outcome to a credible control. Without the control, you cannot separate persuasion from coincidence, seasonality, brand demand or customer intent that already existed.

The counterfactual is where the truth starts to become useful.

## Testing creates budget courage

Incrementality does not just expose waste. It gives teams confidence to fund channels that look weak in attribution but create real demand. This matters most for upper-funnel media, creative testing and channels with longer purchase cycles.

The goal is not to make every decision perfect. It is to make the next budget conversation more honest.

## Start small and repeat

Geo tests, holdouts and audience split tests can all work when the design is clean enough for the decision at hand. Start with one meaningful budget question. Define the success metric, minimum detectable effect and action you will take before the test begins.

A test without a decision is just expensive reporting.
