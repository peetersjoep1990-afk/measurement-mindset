Marketing mix modeling and multi-touch attribution are often treated as rivals. That framing wastes time. They answer different questions, operate at different levels and fail in different ways.

The better question is how to make them work together.

## MTA is tactical and granular

Attribution can help diagnose journeys, landing pages, keyword groups, creative variants and bottom-funnel paths. It is useful for short feedback loops where tracking is strong and the decision is close to the customer interaction.

Its weakness is that it often mistakes observability for importance.

## MMM is strategic and aggregate

Marketing mix modeling can estimate contribution across channels, pricing, seasonality, promotions and external factors. It is better suited to budget allocation and long-term media planning.

Its weakness is that it can be too slow or too broad for everyday optimization.

## Combine models around decisions

Use MMM for budget ranges and channel-level direction. Use incrementality to calibrate causal assumptions. Use MTA for operational optimization inside the channels where it has enough signal. Then document the disagreements instead of smoothing them away.

Disagreement between models is not noise. It is often the most valuable part of the system.
